# 🌀 PORTAL PALS — franchise IP strategy v1

**What this is:** the executive plan for building *Portal Pals* — the animated
kids' universe adapted from the *Changing History* game design
(`/changinghistory/WORLD.md`) — as a game-first entertainment franchise.
The game is not a tie-in to the show; the game **is the IP engine** that
births the show, the film, the toy line, and eventually location-based
entertainment. Every phase must (a) stand alone as a win and (b) generate
the audience + data that de-risks and finances the next phase.

**Operating rule (inherited from the house method):** rigor scales with
spend. No phase is greenlit on faith — each has a numeric gate fed by the
previous phase's telemetry. Small gates = small, cheap failures.

---

## 0 · Why this script

*Changing History* already contains what franchises usually spend years
retrofitting:

1. **A repeatable episode formula.** "What if [famous event] went the other
   way?" → 30 seconds of play → a funny mutated present. That's not just a
   game loop; it's a cartoon cold-open, a toy-line theme, and a dark-ride
   scene, all from one canon database of events.
2. **A collectible layer designed in from day one.** Museum exhibits,
   Famous Figures, Timeline Cards, World Wonders — these are trading cards
   and blind-box toys that already exist as game data.
3. **A parent's co-sign built into the brand.** FUN → CURIOSITY → LEARNING
   (never the reverse) is the moral spine that makes this *Magic School Bus ×
   Pokémon* instead of another disposable kids' brand. Parents buy what they
   trust; kids keep what's fun.
4. **Combinatorial worlds.** Tag-stacking ("Dino-Roman Cyber Empire") gives
   the franchise infinite renewable settings without new lore debt — every
   season, toy wave, and park land is a new tag combo.

## 0.5 · The adaptation: from Changing History to Portal Pals

*Changing History* is the systems layer. *Portal Pals* adds the **character
layer** kids attach to — and the character design writes itself, because the
game already has exactly five mini-game engines:

| Pal (archetype) | Engine | Verb | Toy/story identity |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Sharpshot | AIM | drag-aim-launch | gadget slingshot, precision hero |
| The Dasher | DODGE | steer & survive | the speedster, kinetic comedy |
| The Beat | TIMING | hit the green zone | rhythm kid, cool under pressure |
| The Tank | MASH | tap-to-power | big heart, big energy |
| The Brain | SEQUENCE | watch & repeat | memory whiz, pattern seer |

Five Pals = five play styles = five personalities = five figures on the
shelf. Kids pick a "main" the way they pick a starter Pokémon — and the game
telemetry tells us which Pal is winning *before* we cast a show or tool a toy.

Add two franchise anchors (to be developed in the world bible, gate 1):
- **A portal-creature mascot** — the plush/icon that doesn't age (the
  Pikachu slot). Non-verbal, expressive, lives in every scene transition.
- **A foil** — an antagonist who wants history *erased* while the Pals keep
  it playfully, chaotically *alive*. The conflict is curiosity vs. blankness,
  which keeps the educational spine dramatic instead of preachy.

Canon lives in **one database** (events, tags, figures, combos, consequence
lines). Game, shorts, series, cards, and park scenes all read from it. That
single source of truth is the franchise's real asset.

## The phase ladder

Each phase: **the win it must be on its own → what it proves → the gate that
unlocks the next phase.** Gate numbers are directional targets to be locked
with current category benchmarks at greenlight; the structure is the point.

### PHASE 1 — Secure & seed (weeks, not months)

Begin here. Before a single new asset is built:

- **Chain of title.** Lock rights to the *Changing History* design and all
  derivative works, in writing, worldwide, all media.
- **Trademark "Portal Pals"** across the classes that matter later so we
  never negotiate against ourselves: video games (9/41), toys/games (28),
  entertainment services (41), apparel (25), amusement park services (41).
- **Franchise bible v1** (`portalpals/WORLD.md`, per Game Factory stage 1):
  the five Pals, mascot, foil, tone rules, the FUN→CURIOSITY→LEARNING law,
  and the canon database schema.
- **Win on its own:** an ownable, defensible IP position for the cost of
  legal fees and a writers'-room week.
- **Gate → Phase 2:** bible approved; marks filed; title chain clean.

### PHASE 2 — The game (the flagship market test)

Ship *Portal Pals* the video game — browser + mobile, free, built on the
proven Changing History engine (reskin the systems, recast the fiction
around the Pals). This is the cheapest possible audience test a franchise
has ever had: the vertical slice already exists.

- **Scope:** the existing 8-era map and 5 engines, re-fictionalized; each
  event fronted by a Pal; Timeline Reveal becomes the signature shareable
  beat; Museum becomes the Pals' clubhouse trophy room.
- **Instrument everything** (Game Factory stage 5): starts, D1/D7 return,
  share rate, session length — **plus the franchise question: which Pal do
  kids pick, replay, and share?** Character affinity telemetry is the data
  no focus group can buy.
- **Win on its own:** a live, low-cost game earning attention (and modest
  revenue via cosmetics/season pass per the existing economy design) that is
  profitable at web-game scale.
- **Gate → Phase 3:** directional targets — D1 ≥ 30%, D7 ≥ 10%, median
  session ≥ 6 min, share/challenge rate ≥ 3%, and a clear Pal-affinity
  signal. Miss = iterate the game, not escalate the spend.

### PHASE 3 — The content flywheel (animated shorts)

The Timeline Reveal is a punchline machine: every game event converts to a
60–90 second animated short ("What if the Library of Alexandria never
burned?"). Launch a YouTube/YouTube Kids channel; one short per week,
mirroring the game's weekly event drop — **the game and channel feed each
other from the same canon database.**

- Shorts end where the game begins: "You fix it — play the event now."
  Installs are the measurable click-through.
- Cheap animation tier first (2D, limited rigs); art-style test across the
  first batch, let watch-through pick the look the series will inherit.
- **Win on its own:** self-funding channel (ad revenue + UA value of
  installs) and a growing subscriber base — a media asset even if nothing
  else follows.
- **Gate → Phase 4:** directional — 50%+ avg. retention on shorts, 100k+
  subscribers, measurable short→game install lift, and cumulative organic
  views in the tens of millions. This package *is* the series pitch.

### PHASE 4 — The series (someone else's balance sheet)

Pitch streamers/broadcast with what no pilot script has: MAU, watch-through,
subscriber growth, and character-affinity data proving which Pals kids love.
We are not selling a premise; we are licensing a proven audience.

- Format: 11-minute episodes, two per block — the proven kids-TV shape and
  a natural fit for one-event-one-episode structure.
- Keep game + shorts running through the series window; every episode's
  event ships simultaneously as a playable game drop (the franchise's
  "watch it, then play it" ritual — our structural moat).
- **Win on its own:** a licensed series is revenue-positive by construction
  (the streamer pays); the brand gets mass reach.
- **Gate → Phase 5:** season 1 completion metrics + season 2 renewal, and
  game MAU growth during the airing window (the flywheel must demonstrably
  spin both ways).

### PHASE 5 — Merchandise & syndication (the profit engine)

Toys enter **after** kids know the characters — never before (the graveyard
of toy-first franchises is full). The line is already designed, because the
game's collectibles are the SKUs:

- **Wave 1:** five Pal figures + mascot plush + the Portal Playset (the
  anchor toy: figures go in, "alternate timeline" accessories come out).
- **Wave 2:** blind-box **Artifacts from Changed Timelines** (dinosaur
  currency, Caesar's smartphone, Viking coffee cup — straight from the
  Museum data) and the **Timeline Cards TCG** (tag-stacking = the combo
  mechanic; the game's generator literally balances the card pool).
- **Toy↔game handshake:** each toy carries a code unlocking its in-game
  twin — connected-play uplift without hardware risk.
- Test SKUs in limited retail first; scale on sell-through, not hope.
- **Win on its own:** licensing minimum guarantees make this cash-positive
  before a single unit ships broadly.
- **Gate → Phase 6:** test-market sell-through ≥ 65%, repeat-purchase signal
  on blind-box/TCG lines, series renewed.

### PHASE 6 — The film (the event, not the foundation)

A theatrical (or premium streaming) feature is the franchise's coronation —
greenlit only when series + toys prove durable multi-season demand. The film
premise is the franchise's biggest promise kept: all the timelines collide
(the tag-stacking system at cinematic scale — the Dino-Roman Cyber Empire on
screen).

- Co-finance/partner structure; the IP holder's leverage is the audience
  data and merchandise run-rate, so we trade the least equity at this stage,
  not the most.
- **Win on its own:** the film is a marketing event that resets the toy line
  and game MAU even at box-office break-even.
- **Gate → Phase 7:** film opening + a demonstrated multi-year brand
  (3–5 years of sustained engagement), because parks underwrite decades.

### PHASE 7 — Location-based entertainment (the compounding asset)

Two-step, cheap-first:

1. **The Museum of Changed History — traveling exhibit.** The game's Museum
   is already a walkable attraction concept, and its educational spine makes
   science centers and museums a low-capex, brand-safe beachhead. Kids walk
   through the timelines they created; every exhibit is a photo/share moment.
2. **The Portal Pals dark ride / land.** License to established park
   operators: riders portal through eras; the finale is a real-time
   "timeline reveal" generated from choices made on-ride — the game's core
   system as a physical experience. Each new land expansion = a new tag combo.

- **Win on its own:** licensed LBE is fee-based; the exhibit tour is
  near-term revenue plus a rolling brand billboard.

## The connective tissue (what makes each phase validate the next)

1. **One canon database.** Events, tags, figures, combos live in one place;
   game, shorts, series, cards, and ride scenes are all *renderers* of the
   same data. New content ships everywhere at once for the cost of a data
   row — the Changing History insight, franchised.
2. **Telemetry before greenlights.** Every phase instruments the audience;
   no phase is bought with projections when it can be bought with data.
3. **Everything is a win standalone.** Game profits at web scale; channel
   self-funds; series is streamer-financed; merch is licensed with minimum
   guarantees; film is co-financed at maximum leverage; parks are fee-based.
   There is no phase where the franchise bets itself.
4. **The ritual moat.** "Watch the what-if, then play it yourself" is the
   loop no competitor inherits by copying a character design.
5. **FUN → CURIOSITY → LEARNING** is non-negotiable at every phase gate —
   it is the reason parents let this brand into the house, which is the only
   durable distribution channel in kids' entertainment.

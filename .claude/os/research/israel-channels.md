# Research: Israel + diaspora distribution channels (sourced)

## WhatsApp = the railroad
- **98–99% of Israelis use WhatsApp**; 95% daily. Avg Israeli is in **~15 groups** (IDI 2024).
- **65% of Israelis get news from WhatsApp**; >60% trust their family/friends groups for news.
- Telegram: 54%→70% of adults (post-Oct-7 jump); ~2.5M weekly actives.
- Other: YouTube 97%, Facebook 88% (~half of social web visits), Instagram 81% (4.65M), TikTok 57% (4.16M 18+). Since Oct 7, TikTok use > all other socials combined (Ynet).

## Press that demonstrably covers viral Jewish/Israeli games
The Hebrew-Wordle moment mapped the whole ecosystem: **one JTA wire story ran in ~10 outlets within days.**
- **JTA** (syndicates to ~60 Jewish outlets) · **The Forward** (2M+ readers/mo, built its own Yiddish Wordle "Vertl") · **Times of Israel** (8M uniques/mo claimed, >half US traffic; covered Meduyeket + Jewdle) · **Jerusalem Post** (regular Israeli-indie features) · **Haaretz** (hosts its OWN Hebrew word games — the format is mainstream) · **Hey Alma** (covers Jewish games as a beat; 70 Faces Media = 3M/mo reach, 350K daily email subs) · **Ynet** (#1, ~65M visits/mo, 61% weekly reach) · **Geektime** (dedicated indie-games tag) · **Calcalist/CTech** · Israeli Indie Games Newsletter (indiegames.co.il) · GameIS/MakeGamesJLM community.

## Precedents
- **Meduyeket** (Hebrew Wordle): built solo, launched Jan 15 2022, thousands of dailies within ~10 days, international press.
- **Jewdle** (Shalom org): Jewish-identity Wordle → near-total diaspora press saturation in days. Creator's stated play: "Jewish relevance within a popular secular context."
- Haaretz 2026 feature: Israeli game made during the Iran war as 90s-childhood nostalgia — wartime-context + nostalgia games are a human-interest beat.
- Iron Dome iOS game (2014): shipped during war, drew press, **removed by Apple within a week** — app-store policy risk; web distribution avoids it.

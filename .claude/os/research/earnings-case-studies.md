# Research: real web-game earnings (verified case studies)

## The honest ladder (verified numbers)
- **Floor (portal revshare, simple games):** DonislawDev — 451K CrazyGames plays →
  ~**€0.8–1.0 per 1,000 plays**; called it "a big fail." (donislawdev.com, primary)
- **Self-hosted + gaming ad network:** Imposter Game (solo dev, 2025–26) — 130K MAU
  peak, $0 marketing; AdSense $145 then banned → Venatus **$1,280 over 3 months
  (~$425/mo at 50K MAU ≈ $8–9 per 1,000 MAU/mo)**. (dev.to/yitao postmortem, primary)
- **GolfRoyale.io:** 120K players lifetime → ~€400 total; sold site+code+Discord
  for $1,500. (indiehackers, primary)
- **Licensing era benchmark:** True Valhalla — HTML5 license income peaked
  **$12–14K/mo** (2015–16), ~$500/non-exclusive license; htmlgames.com sells
  non-exclusive licenses at **$1,000/game** today.
- **Scale winners:** Shell Shockers — **"low seven figures annually," 80–90% ads**,
  200M accounts (founder on record). Smash Karts: 70M+ players, 70–80% ad revenue,
  3-person team full-time. Mope.io sold **>$1M cash** (2020). 1v1.LOL acquired
  **$42M** (SEC filing, 2022). Krunker.io reported $40M (press).
- **Wordle:** $0 revenue, no ads → sold "low seven figures." Distribution itself
  was the asset.

## Poki trajectory datapoints
- Artem Lanin/Playrea: 25 failed mobile games → 5 Poki games, **67M gameplays in
  2025**; Brainrot Puzzle 1M plays day one; now his main occupation.
- Poki curation: ~**one new game per day** accepted; ranking = avg playtime +
  click-to-play conversion, not spend.

## Planning numbers for Miklat (synthesis, flagged)
- Portal plays: budget **~$1–3 per 1,000 plays** until proven otherwise.
- Self-hosted with a gaming network: **~$5–10 per 1,000 MAU/mo**.
- Israel-geo ads ≈ **35–50% of US rates** (YouTube IL $14 vs US $33; Meta IL $8.4
  vs US $23 — cross-channel proxy). Diaspora (US) traffic is worth 2–3× Israeli
  traffic per impression.
- Tip conversion: assume **<1% of players**; avg tip $3–5.
- First-game realistic band once portal-distributed: **$500–3K/mo** (top of funnel
  stories; floor anecdotes much lower).

# Research: web-game ad economics (2024–2026, sourced)

## Real web-game numbers (not mobile-SDK marketing tables)
- golfroyale.io (120K players, AdinPlay): video **≈€1.20 CPM**, banner **≈€0.25**;
  blended ≈ **€3 per 1,000 players**. (indiehackers.com, older post — pre-Venatus demand)
- CPMStar floor case: **$0.17 CPM**. AdSense gaming-niche page RPM: **$2–8** (ranktracker 2025).
- Synthesis (flagged estimate): self-hosted web game tier-1 traffic ≈
  **$5–20 per 1,000 sessions** with a gaming ad partner; AdSense-display-only $2–8;
  tier-2/3 traffic 5–10× lower and fill-constrained.

## Mobile benchmarks for contrast (do NOT promise these on web)
- Rewarded video US: **$16–20 eCPM** (Appodeal Q4 2024). Interstitial US: **~$14**.
  Banner US: **~$0.68**. Web runs well below: est. $1–6 interstitial / $3–12 rewarded tier-1.

## Networks
- **AdinPlay** (browser-game specialist, acquired by Venatus 2023): revshare
  negotiated, not public; application-based. Venatus claims +50–95% RPM vs
  AdSense (vendor claim). One Trustpilot non-payment complaint (flag).
- **Google H5 Games Ads** (beta): interstitial + rewarded only, normal AdSense
  account, standard ~68% share, by-application, no published minimum (but
  practically favors big traffic; at times closed).

## Geo
- Israel ≈ AdSense RPM **~$3** vs US ~$6.5 → **roughly 40–60% of US rates**
  (high tier-2/low tier-1; partnerkin table, flagged inference).
- Fill: rewarded tier-1 >95%; generic networks 25–55%; fill matters more than
  headline CPM ($5 eCPM × 60% fill < $4.50 × 85%).

## Revenue reality checks
- First web game: **$500–3,000/mo** (indiegamebusiness.com).
- 130K-MAU web game postmortem: ads+subs = servers + "healthy profit" after
  moving AdSense→Venatus (dev.to/yitao).
- Puckit! on CrazyGames: 100K plays, revenue under NDA; 4m28s avg session.

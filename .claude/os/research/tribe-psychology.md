# Research: "support your tribe" — psychology + quantified cases (sourced)

## Post-Oct-7 Jewish/Israeli solidarity money (the thesis is real)
- **Israel Bonds: $1B in ~4 weeks, $3B+ within 6 months** (~triple normal pace; 72-yr record). Many first-time buyers citing solidarity.
- Charitable donations to Israel: **$1.4B+** (gov report, Apr 2024).
- Israeli wine US importer +40% volume/revenue 2024 ("Sip for Solidarity").
- Star of David jewelry: individual sellers +300–600%; Judaica ~+20% (anecdotal aggregate, flag).
- Wartime domestic buy-local shift (2025): ACE +75%, Delta +54%, Terminal X +49%, Fox/KSP +30% orders.
- **Eurovision 2024: Israel 2nd in televote (323 pts) vs 52 jury pts** — diaspora mobilization moves coordinated public votes.
- Asymmetry warning: boycott apps dwarf buycott apps (400K vs 12K users on Buycott platform, 2014) — pro-side support is high-intent but needs to be made EASY.

## Black-owned business movement (the mechanism design lesson)
- Yelp "Black-owned" searches +7,043% (2020). **AER 2025 study: the LABEL alone → +67% online traffic, ~+10% foot traffic** — visibility converts sentiment to revenue.
- 15% Pledge: one Instagram post → Sephora etc.; 385 brands shelved in year 1; ~$14B claimed redirected (self-reported, flag).
- McKinsey: 63% want to support, **only 23% know how to find them** → discovery is the bottleneck, not desire.
- UW counter-study: unsupported spikes fade — solidarity converts to retention only if the product holds.
- **Black Card Revoked**: culture-specific trivia game — 800 sold day one, ~$250K presales in 72h, ~$2M/yr, BET show. Direct precedent for culturally-specific casual gaming as a business.
- CultureTags: six-figure sales in 9 months.

## K-pop ARMY (mobilization mechanics to copy)
- #MatchAMillion: **$1M matched in <24h from 35,609 donors**. What made it work: pre-existing volunteer infrastructure, concrete numeric target, live public tally, hashtag, deadline urgency, tiny per-person ask ("one dollar per fan").

## Academic anchors
- **Tajfel minimal group paradigm**: even arbitrary group identity produces in-group favoritism — people sacrifice absolute gain for relative tribal advantage.
- **CETSCALE (Shimp & Sharma 1987)**: ethnocentric consumers prefer in-group goods even at lower quality.
- **Diaspora marketing (HBR 2013)**: target "ethnic affirmers" + "biculturals"; biculturals are highest-value because they bridge to the mainstream.
- **Muniz & O'Guinn brand community**: kinship-consciousness + shared rituals + moral responsibility → loyalty/advocacy.
- **PENS/Self-Determination (Ryan/Rigby/Przybylski)**: psychological need-fit (autonomy, competence, RELATEDNESS) predicts engagement — not graphical fidelity.
